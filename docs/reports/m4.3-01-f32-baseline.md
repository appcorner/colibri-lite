## Numerical Diagnosis and F64

The first `single_low_token` pass exceeded the largest prior M4.2
fixture-specific logit value. The required same-input diagnostic found an
all-logit PyTorch-F32-versus-Rust maximum of `1.9073486328125e-5`. Selected
normal-reference versus same-input effects reached `2.7370452880859375e-4`,
so the larger end-to-end difference is incoming accumulated state drift, not a
local LM-head defect.

Tier B budgets are newly scoped to each named fixture using the ADR 0025
formulas. They do not alter the M4.2 registry.

Six selected F64 records cover RMSNorm reductions/outputs and LM-head dot
products. Rust F32 is closer in two cases and Transformers F32 in four. This is
diagnostic-only and changes no arithmetic or tolerance contract. The complete
model was not executed in F64.

## Future Comparison and Gate

Future variants are classified as exact-equivalent, numerically equivalent
within contract, semantically equivalent, quality-risk, or correctness
failure. Every variant must report correctness, numerical-contract, memory,
I/O, latency/throughput, artifact-size, and platform/dependency deltas.

Identity, deterministic router semantics, frozen generated IDs, safe discrete
selections, structural intermediate evidence, finite outputs, bounded cache
behavior, and evidence compatibility are mandatory. Memory, size, reads,
latency, throughput, and hit rate are tradeable only after mandatory invariants
pass. Speed alone cannot accept a change.
